Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, the "Missile Man of India," was a man of extraordinary qualities. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and he dedicated his life to the betterment of India.

Notable Achievements

HE HELPED BUILD INDIA'S FIRST INDIGENOUS HOVERCRAFT

successful in developing India's first indigenous hovercraft which was named Nandi, the impressive white bull who acts as the vehicle of the Hindu deity Lord Shiva.

KALAM WAS THE PROJECT DIRECTOR OF INDIA'S FIRST SLV

On July 18, 1980, Satellite Launch Vehicle-3 (SLV-3) was successfully launched from Sriharikota range in the Nellore district of Andhra Pradesh. It deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit. It was the first successful satellite launch that took place on Indian soil and thereby made India the seventh member of an exclusive club of space-faring nations in international society.

HE WAS RESPONSIBLE FOR THE SUCCESSFUL LAUNCH OF AGNI AND PRITHVI MISSILES

After years of consistent hard work and immense dedication, India got her first range of ballistic missiles, the Prithvi, the Agni, the Aakash, and the Nag.

HE SUPERVISED THE POKHRAN-II EXPLOSIONS AS THE CHIEF OF DRDO

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) from July 1992 to December 1999.

HE SERVED AS THE FIRST PRINCIPAL SCIENTIFIC ADVISOR TO THE GOVERNMENT OF INDIA

After his retirement from DRDO, Kalam was appointed as the first-ever Principal Scientific Advisor (PSA) to the Government Of India from November 1999 to November 2001.

HE SERVED AS THE 11TH PRESIDENT OF INDIA

On June 10, 2002, the National Democratic Alliance (NDA), the then ruling party expressed their will to nominate Dr. Kalam for the post of President.He served as the 11th President of India from July 25, 2002 to July 25, 2007.

HE WROTE THE BESTSELLER WINGS OF FIRE

Dr Kalam had always encouraged the young population of India to develop the habit of reading and he penned down many inspirational books that always carried a message for his countrymen and addressed burning issues relevant to India.

HE WAS AWARDED THE BHARAT RATNA IN 1997

Dr Kalam was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 by the Government of India.

Some of the Motivational Quotes

What set Dr. Kalam apart was his passion for education. He believed that education had the power to transform lives and nations. He was an inspirational teacher, encouraging and inspiring millions of young minds to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals. His vision extended beyond science and technology; it encompassed the holistic development of individuals and society.

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's legacy continues to inspire people worldwide. Although he passed away on July 27, 2015, his teachings and values live on. He remains a shining example of leadership, integrity, and dedication, serving as an inspiration for generations to come.
